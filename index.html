EC BUYING PROCESS
Step-by-step timeline of purchasing an Executive Condominium
Step 1 — Sales Launch
Project details, pricing and unit mix released.

Prepare:
Check eligibility
Secure loan approval
Budget for downpayment, stamp duty & legal fees
Account for resale levy (if applicable)

Payment: None
Step 2 — Submit Application
Apply directly with the developer.

Units may be allocated via:
Balloting system
First-come-first-serve

Payment: Check with developer
Step 3 — Application Outcome
Developer informs you if you are successful.

Payment: None
Step 4 — Book Unit
Receive Option To Purchase (OTP).

Submit CPF Housing Grant documents if applicable.

Payment:
5% option fee (cash / cheque / transfer)
Step 5 — Sign Sale & Purchase Agreement
Engage a solicitor and confirm financing.

Payment:
15% downpayment (CPF or cash)
Stamp duty
Legal fees
Step 6 — Key Collection
Collect keys upon project completion.

Payment: Final payments via loan / CPF
AFTER BUYING YOUR EC
Key rules and restrictions you must follow
Minimum Occupation Period (MOP)
You must live in the EC for 5 years.

During this period, you cannot:
Sell the unit
Rent out the entire unit
Property Ownership Restriction
During the 5-year MOP:
Cannot own any private residential property
After 5 Years
You may sell your EC on the open market.

Eligible buyers:
Singapore Citizens (SC)
Singapore Permanent Residents (PR)
After 10 Years
EC becomes fully privatized.

You can:
Sell to foreigners
Treat it like a private condominium
Resale Levy
Applies if you previously bought subsidised housing.

Payable upon EC purchase
HDB Disposal Rule
If you own an HDB flat:
Must sell within 6 months after EC completion
Always verify final eligibility and conditions with the property developer, HDB, or your solicitor.
